Chef Johns Salmon

Description:

Chef John's Salmon is a delightful dish that showcases the natural flavors of fresh salmon complemented by a flavorful herb spread. To prepare this dish, two 8-ounce center-cut salmon fillets, complete with skin, are seasoned with a touch of kosher salt. The fillets are then placed skin side down on a foil-lined baking sheet that has been lightly brushed with vegetable oil. The herbaceous magic begins with a paste made from sliced garlic, fresh tarragon, and flat-leaf parsley, creating a burst of aromatic flavors. Chef John's special herb spread, consisting of mayonnaise, Dijon mustard, fresh lemon juice, and a hint of cayenne pepper, is generously spooned over the top and sides of each salmon fillet. The cooking process involves a two-step approach: first, a quick broil under high heat for approximately 5 minutes to achieve a beautifully browned exterior, followed by a gentle bake at 350 degrees F until the internal temperature reaches a perfect 130 degrees F. This results in salmon that flakes easily with a fork, showcasing a harmonious blend of textures and flavors. Chef John's Salmon is not only a culinary masterpiece but also a testament to the art of balancing simplicity with sophistication in seafood preparation.

    Ingredients:
  • 2 (8 ounce) center-cut salmon fillets, with skin
  • Â¼ teaspoon kosher salt
  • 1 teaspoon vegetable oil 1 clove garlic
  • sliced 1 tablespoon chopped fresh tarragon
  • 1 tablespoon chopped fresh flat-leaf parsley
  • 3 tablespoons mayonnaise
  • 1 teaspoon Dijon mustard
  • 1 teaspoon fresh lemon juice
  • 1 pinch cayenne pepper
    Steps:
  1. Step 1: Season salmon fillets with kosher salt. Line a baking baking sheet with foil and brush lightly with vegetable oil.
  2. Step 2: Preheat oven's broiler on high and set the oven rack about 8 inches from the heat source.
  3. Step 3: Process garlic, tarragon, and parsley in a blender or mortar and pestle to form a loose paste.
  4. Step 4: Mix mayonnaise, Dijon mustard, lemon juice, and cayenne pepper into garlic paste until combined.
  5. Step 5: Place salmon fillets skin side down on the baking sheet. Spoon herb spread over the top and sides of each fillet.
  6. Step 6: Cook under the preheated broiler until fillets are well-browned, about 5 minutes. Turn the broiler off and turn the oven to 350 degrees F (175 degrees C).
  7. Step 7: Bake until the internal temperature of the salmon is 130 degrees F (55 degrees C) and salmon flakes easily with a fork, about 3 to 4 minutes.
